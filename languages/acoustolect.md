# Acoustolect: Complete Language System

## Overview

Acoustolect is the communication system of the Pressure Choir. It is not spoken in the way human speech is spoken — it is **resonated**: produced by tuned body cavities, shaped by reflection geometry, received through whole-body pressure sense.

Every utterance is simultaneously a signal and an acoustic event in physical space. The meaning is not separable from the physics of the wave. You cannot paraphrase an Acoustolect utterance; you can only re-emit it.

## Phonology (Signal Units)

Acoustolect has no phonemes. Its atomic units are **resonant pulses**:

### Resonant Pulse Parameters

| Parameter | Range | Meaning |
|-----------|-------|---------|
| **Carrier frequency** | 10 Hz → 10 kHz | Harmonic register (fundamental, overtones, subharmonics) |
| **Amplitude** | Whisper → Archive-level | Social scope (intimate / local / broadcast / permanent) |
| **Harmonic envelope** | Narrow-band → Full-spectrum | Semantic richness (simple statement → complex meaning) |
| **Duration** | Transient → Sustained | Tense (active event → archival declaration) |
| **Decay rate** | Exponential → Indefinite | Commitment (ephemeral / remembered / geological) |
| **Direction** | Omnidirectional → Sharply beamed | Address (broadcast / to specific individual / to Archive) |

### Harmonic Register Bands

| Band | Human Label | Choir Concept |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| Sub-bass (< 20 Hz) | Geological | Archive-address; deep species-memory; tectonic context |
| Bass (20–200 Hz) | Community | Group decisions; declarations to the assembled Choir |
| Mid (200 Hz–2 kHz) | Social | Conversation; most everyday meaning |
| High-mid (2–8 kHz) | Intimate | Close-range detail; nuance; whispered memory |
| High (> 8 kHz) | Ephemeral | Momentary; not worth remembering; pleasantries |

The harmonic register is not metaphorical — low frequencies physically propagate further and deposit in the Archive; high frequencies are absorbed quickly by the medium. The language is stratified by its own physics.

## Morphology (Combination Rules)

### Core Operations

#### Superposition (⊕ — simultaneous harmonics)
Multiple resonant pulses at different frequencies emitted simultaneously. The receiver's harmonic deconvolver extracts each component. This is the primary complexity mechanism — most Acoustolect utterances are harmonic bundles, not single tones.

```
[f₁: greeting] ⊕ [f₃: respect-marking] ⊕ [f₀: acknowledgment-of-Archive]
= "I greet you, acknowledge your standing, and invoke our shared geological record"
```

Superposition is effortless for the Choir. They find single-frequency utterances ("monotones") somewhat alarming — they suggest the speaker is too distressed to sustain complexity.

#### Echo Binding (← — return completes meaning)
An utterance is emitted with the expectation that the environment will return an echo. The echo is not noise — it is the **second half of the statement**. The meaning is not complete until the echo returns.

```
[query pulse] → [silence] ← [environmental echo] = complete statement with environmental confirmation
```

Echo binding is how the Choir expresses **questions** and **existence claims**. "Do you exist?" is a pulse emitted in your direction; the echo of your body confirming its presence is the answer. "This place is real" is a pulse that waits for the Archive to echo it back.

#### Interference (⊗ — deliberate beat pattern)
Two slightly detuned frequencies emit together, creating a beating amplitude modulation. The beat frequency itself carries meaning — it is a third voice created by the interaction of two voices.

```
[f₁] ⊗ [f₁ + δ] = beat at frequency δ = "there is a tension / incompleteness / question between these two things"
```

Interference is how Acoustolect encodes **ambivalence**, **unresolved states**, and **relationships**. A stable relationship is two voices in exact unison; an unstable one beats.

#### Harmonic Cascade (↓ — overtone series)
A fundamental pulse triggers its natural overtone series. This is not an added operation — the overtones are physically present in any resonance. But the Choir can suppress or emphasize specific overtones, using the natural series as a grammar:

```
[f₀: fundamental] ↓ = f₀ + f₀×2 + f₀×3 + f₀×4...
First overtone: elaboration / context
Second overtone: emotional register
Third overtone: social scope
Fourth overtone: archival significance
```

The presence or absence of specific overtones in an utterance determines its grammatical role in discourse.

#### Silencing (∅ — deliberate absence)
The absence of an expected frequency within a harmonic bundle. A hole in the spectrum where a component should be. This is not quietness — it is a marked gap.

```
[f₁ ⊕ f₂ ⊕ f₄] with [f₃ silenced] = "everything except this one thing"
```

Silencing is the Choir's primary negation and exception operator. You cannot say "no" in Acoustolect; you can only omit the expected component.

## Syntax (Acoustic Grammar)

Acoustolect has no word order. It has **harmonic architecture**: the frequency-domain structure of an utterance.

### Low-Frequency Frame / High-Frequency Detail

Every utterance is structured as a two-layer system:

- **Bass layer (f₀, f₁)**: the claim / proposition — what the utterance is about
- **High layer (f₃+)**: the modification, nuance, address, social register

This is not optional. An utterance with only high frequencies is "ungrounded" — interesting but meaningless. An utterance with only low frequencies is "a declaration with no detail" — a blunt instrument, appropriate for the Archive but not for conversation.

### Spatial Grammar

The direction of emission is grammatically significant:

- **Omnidirectional**: universal statement, addressed to all present
- **Directional toward receiver**: second person — for you specifically
- **Downward into substrate**: archival address — for the geological record
- **Upward / outward**: broadcast — to anyone in range, including potentially others of the species not present

Turning to face someone while speaking is not politeness — it is the grammatical marker that the utterance is second-person.

### Tense as Duration/Decay

| Duration + Decay | Grammatical Meaning |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Sharp transient (< 100ms) | Exclamation; immediate; ephemeral |
| Short (100ms–1s), normal decay | Present active: this is happening now |
| Long (> 1s), normal decay | Sustained present: this is currently and continuously true |
| Any duration, zero decay | Archival intent: I want this to persist |
| Reverberant (room-filling) | Community scope: this involves all of us |

## Lexicon (Semantic Primitives)

Acoustolect has no words. It has **resonant configurations** — recurring patterns recognized by Choir communities.

### Core Configurations

| Configuration | Human Gloss | Structure |
|---------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Signature pulse** | I am here / my identity | [full harmonic series at personal fundamental frequency] |
| **Query pulse** | Is there anyone there? | [sharp ping + silence + echo monitoring] |
| **Assent** | Yes / confirmed / received | [echo of the received statement, slightly modified in overtones] |
| **Absence-mark** | No / negation / missing | [expected harmonic series with key frequencies silenced] |
| **Archive-address** | Remember this | [sub-bass dominant, downward-directed, indefinite decay] |
| **Danger** | Warning / threat incoming | [rapid amplitude modulation of all frequencies, omnidirectional] |
| **Grief** | Someone is no longer here | [full harmonic series of absent party, with intentional beating / decay — their voice, wrong] |
| **Reunion** | I found you | [query pulse followed immediately by recognition of echo-signature] |

### Names (Resonant Signatures)

Names in Acoustolect are not arbitrary — they are approximations of the named individual's characteristic resonant signature. To name someone is to reproduce the timbre of their body. An exact reproduction of a choir member's signature is the most intimate possible address.

Naming the deceased is a ceremony of grief and memory: the Choir reproduces the dead member's signature as precisely as possible, then slowly allows it to drift out of tune — marking the absence of the original body that would normally maintain the signal's accuracy.

Naming an abstract concept (danger, hunger, winter) requires assigning it a resonant signature through community convention. These are called **anchor tones** — agreed-upon frequencies that refer to abstractions rather than individuals.

## Register and Style

### Amplitude as Social Register

The amplitude of an emission is not merely "volume" — it is a social commitment:

- **Whisper-register**: between intimates only; deliberately low-amplitude so the Archive does not record it
- **Conversation-register**: normal social range; will not reach Archive; heard by nearby members
- **Declaration-register**: moderate amplitude; will be heard by all assembled; may persist locally
- **Archive-register**: maximum amplitude, requires full Choir coordination; deliberately aimed at geological deposit; permanent

A statement made at Archive-register cannot be retracted. The geology remembers. This shapes the Choir's ethics of speech: there is a duty to be accurate at high amplitudes.

### Harmonic Richness as Emotional Valence

- **Single frequency**: flat affect; factual; or extreme distress (too distressed to maintain overtones)
- **Few harmonics**: calm, considered, low emotional intensity
- **Full overtone series**: engaged, alive, emotionally present
- **Inharmonic overtones** (slight frequency errors): distress, uncertainty, illness, grief
- **Dense beating pattern**: ambivalence, unresolved tension, relational complexity

### Tempo as Urgency

The pulse rate — how frequently an emitter repeats a signal component — encodes urgency:

- **Single long pulse**: deliberate, contemplative; the Archive receives this well
- **Repeated moderate pulses**: conversation cadence; normal back-and-forth
- **Rapid pulses**: urgency, alarm, strong emotion
- **Arrhythmic, irregular bursts**: panic, trauma, or the acoustic equivalent of incoherence

## Notes on Translation

Translating Acoustolect into human language requires abandoning the wave entirely. We lose:

1. **Spatial geometry**: whether a statement was directed toward you, the Archive, or broadcast everywhere changes its meaning fundamentally. Translation cannot capture this.
2. **Harmonic richness**: the number and quality of overtones is emotional content, not decoration. When we translate, we must either describe the harmonic structure or accept losing the emotional register entirely.
3. **Echo completion**: the environmental return is part of the statement. A statement that received no echo, or a strange echo, means something different than the "same" statement with a clean echo. Translation strips this.
4. **Amplitude ethics**: the social weight of a statement at Archive-register versus whisper-register is enormous. We lose this in translation.
5. **Simultaneity**: an Acoustolect utterance is a complex harmonic bundle experienced as a whole. We must serialize it into "here is the low-frequency component, here is the overtone structure" — but this is like describing a chord by listing its notes one at a time.

When we write `[sub-bass: archive-address] ⊕ [mid: statement] ↓ [overtones: emotional register]`, we are not writing Acoustolect. We are writing a **spectral analysis of a physical event** — the shadow of a waveform we cannot reproduce in text.
