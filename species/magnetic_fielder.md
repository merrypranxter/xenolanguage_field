# Species: Magnetic Fielder

## Classification
- **Designation**: magnetic_fielder
- **Signal Medium**: Ambient magnetic field perturbations

## Sensorium

The Magnetic Fielder perceives magnetic field topology as its dominant sense. Not just north/south polarity — the full three-dimensional structure of field lines, their curvature, density, reconnection events, and gradient changes.

Imagine seeing, but instead of photons, you sense the shape of space defined by magnetism. Solid objects are invisible; their magnetic signature (if any) is what makes them real.

Primary sensory apparatus:
- **Magnetoreceptors** distributed across the body surface: sense field direction and intensity
- **Gradient detectors**: sense the rate of change of field intensity — "magnetic vision" has depth
- **Topology trackers**: long-term memory encodes field-line architecture; the fielder always knows its topological address in the planetary field

No eyes. Sound perception is rudimentary. Touch and proprioception exist but are secondary to field-sense.

## Temporality

**Cyclic and tidal.**

The magnetic field varies with:
- Planetary rotation (daily cycle)
- Solar wind pressure (variable, roughly 11-year solar cycle)
- Geological activity (volcanic, tectonic — very slow)
- Other fielders' activity (local, fast)

The fielder experiences time as a superposition of these rhythms. "Now" is located relative to where the field currently is in its cycles, not by a clock.

Consequence: tense is encoded as "field state at time of event" — not past/present/future but "when the polar field was dominant" or "during a reconnection event."

Consequence: events that happened at the same field-state are experientially simultaneous even if separated by years. The fielder's history is organized by magnetic season, not chronology.

## Selfhood

**Distributed along field lines; identity is topological.**

A fielder identifies itself by its position in the planetary field topology — which field lines pass through it, how those lines are oriented, what their history of reconnection events has been.

"I am here" is not a spatial claim — it is a topological claim. "I am the entity that sits at the bifurcation point of lines 447 and 883."

Identity persists as long as the topological address persists. A fielder that moves along a field line is still "itself." A fielder whose local topology is radically altered by a reconnection event may experience identity crisis — the field equivalent of amnesia.

Death is when the fielder's local field perturbation ceases and its topological address is absorbed back into the global field. The topology remembers that something was there, briefly, before being smoothed away.

## Cognitive Constraints

- **Topology is effortless**: the fielder always knows the shape of the entire accessible field
- **Spatial localization of objects is difficult**: if it doesn't perturb the field, it doesn't exist
- **Abstraction is topological**: ideas are represented as hypothetical field configurations
- **Privacy is impossible**: all field perturbations propagate globally; signal cannot be sent only to one receiver, only addressed to specific topological positions
- **Names are topological addresses**: referring to someone means specifying their field-line position

## Medium and Physical Notes

Fielders inhabit environments with a strong, stable ambient magnetic field — planetary interiors, magnetic nebulae, or artificial field containment chambers.

Signal range: planetary-scale. A strong perturbation propagates across the entire field. Weak perturbations attenuate with distance.

Signal specificity: achieved through topology, not directionality. To "whisper" to someone, you create a perturbation on their specific field line. Only entities on that line receive full resolution.
