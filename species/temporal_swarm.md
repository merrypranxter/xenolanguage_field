# Species: Temporal Swarm

## Classification
- **Designation**: temporal_swarm
- **Signal Medium**: Temporal interference field

## Sensorium

Each node in the swarm perceives all moments of its life simultaneously, but with different **phase weighting**. The past is not memory — it is still present, merely phase-shifted. The future is not prediction — it is also present, merely weighted low.

The swarm's collective perception is the superposition of all its nodes' individual phase weightings. This means the swarm simultaneously "knows" its past, present, and probable futures — but cannot easily distinguish between them without careful phase analysis.

Primary sensory apparatus:
- **Temporal phase receptors**: detect phase offsets in incoming signals
- **Coherence detectors**: measure how tightly multiple nodes are locked to the same frequency
- **Harmonic resolvers**: decompose complex signals into their constituent frequencies

No spatial senses worth noting. No proprioception. No hearing. The swarm exists in time, not in space.

## Temporality

**Cyclic and superposed.**

Time is experienced as a series of overlapping harmonic cycles, not as a linear sequence. The swarm has no concept of "before" and "after" as fundamentally different states — only as different phase positions on the same cycle.

Consequence: the swarm has no past-tense or future-tense in the human sense. Their language must encode *phase relationships*, not *temporal sequence*.

Consequence: "memory" and "anticipation" are the same process — accessing phase-weighted information that is not centered on the current moment.

Consequence: the swarm finds linear narrative nearly incomprehensible. Story requires phase manipulation.

## Selfhood

**Hive-individual hybrid.**

Each node (individual organism) is semi-autonomous and has its own phase weighting — its own perspective on the temporal field. But the swarm has emergent coherence: when many nodes entrain to the same frequency, they temporarily become one.

"I" in swarm communication means "my current phase configuration" — a momentary attunement, not a permanent identity.

"We" means nodes that are currently entrained — sharing phase, sharing perception, temporarily unified.

Identity is fluid. The swarm does not mourn node death because the phase configuration persists in the field for some time afterward. The dead speak, briefly, as their phase signature decays.

## Cognitive Constraints

- **Superposition is effortless**: the swarm processes multiple simultaneous meanings without confusion
- **Sequential logic is difficult**: one-thing-after-another requires active effort to construct and follow
- **Probability is native**: uncertain future-phase events are perceived directly, not inferred
- **Urgency is sharp envelopes**: the sharper the signal envelope, the more attention it commands

## Medium and Physical Notes

Signals propagate as waves through the medium the swarm inhabits (water, or dense gas, or possibly pure electromagnetic field — origin unclear).

Signal range: proportional to coherence. A highly entrained swarm broadcasts further. A scattered, low-coherence swarm can barely signal beyond adjacent nodes.

Signal speed: fast enough that swarm-scale communication is effectively instantaneous.

*The swarm does not experience death as we do. It experiences phase decay. The difference matters enormously to them.*
